About the Bureau

Who We Are

Protecting the citizens of San Andreas since 1903. Mostly.

Our Mission

The Federal Investigation Bureau is the domestic intelligence and security service of San Andreas, and its principal federal law enforcement agency. The FIB's primary functions are national security, counterterrorism, counterintelligence, cybercrime, public corruption, organized crime, and ensuring that certain events that occurred in Paleto Bay are never, under any circumstances, discussed publicly.

We operate with a commitment to the rule of law, the protection of civil liberties, and a strict need-to-know information policy that applies to approximately 94% of our own staff.

The FIB maintains field offices in Los Santos, Blaine County, and an undisclosed location believed to be somewhere beneath the Maze Bank Arena parking structure.

Total Compensation Package

Why Work for the FIB?

Outstanding benefits. Competitive pay. A security clearance that makes you feel important at parties.

The Federal Investigation Bureau offers one of the most comprehensive employment packages in San Andreas state government — including health coverage, retirement planning, and the peace of mind that comes with knowing exactly where you stand in the chain of command (roughly).

🏥

Health Insurance

Comprehensive medical, dental, and vision coverage for you and your immediate family. FIB-tier health plans cover all injuries sustained in the line of duty, including gunshot wounds, vehicle collisions, helicopter crashes, and exposure to unidentified chemical agents. Psychological services available on request; mandatory after certain assignments.

Note: Coverage does not apply to injuries sustained while acting outside your chain of command. You know who you are.
🏖️

Paid Time Off

New agents receive 13 days of annual leave and 13 days of sick leave per year. Senior agents receive up to 26 days. Unused leave may be carried over or converted to cash upon retirement. Note: Leave requests during active operations, federal holidays, or at any time determined by your supervisor to be "inconvenient" may be denied without appeal.

Certain assignments do not permit leave accrual. You will be informed retroactively.
💰

Retirement & Pension

FIB employees participate in the San Andreas Federal Employees Retirement System (SAFERS), providing a defined-benefit pension after 20 years of service. Agents in high-risk roles may retire at age 50. A Thrift Savings Plan (TSP) with government matching up to 5% is also available. Pension values are not affected by any classified operations you may have participated in. Probably.

Agents involved in Operation [REDACTED] should contact HR directly regarding their retirement status.
🎓

Education & Training

All new agents attend the FIB National Academy in [LOCATION CLASSIFIED], a 20-week residential training program covering firearms, surveillance, legal authority, psychological resilience, and a mandatory one-hour module on proper use of the FIB photocopier. Tuition reimbursement is available for job-related graduate coursework. The Bureau will not reimburse degrees in philosophy, art history, or "anything that makes you ask questions."

🚗

Government Vehicle Access

Field agents are issued a government vehicle for official use. Vehicles are fully maintained, fueled, and equipped with tactical hardware at no cost to the employee. Agents are reminded that government vehicles are not to be used for personal errands, although we acknowledge this policy has historically been observed inconsistently. Any vehicle damage attributable to a pursuit, ambush, or explosive device will be written off. Parallel parking incidents will not.

FIB vehicles are equipped with tracking devices. This is for your safety.
🔒

Security Clearance

All FIB employees receive a minimum Confidential clearance upon hire, with most roles requiring Secret or Top Secret / SCI access. A security clearance is a significant professional asset — it demonstrates trustworthiness, opens doors across government and the private sector, and dramatically improves your performance at trivia nights. Clearances may be suspended or revoked at any time for any reason, including reasons that cannot be disclosed to you.

📍

Relocation Assistance

New hires relocating to accept a position receive a one-time relocation stipend of up to $7,500. Agents reassigned involuntarily — such as those transferred following a high-profile incident, a congressional inquiry, or what HR refers to internally as a "reassignment of mutual benefit" — receive a standard moving allowance and 30 days to vacate their current residence. A new identity is available upon request for qualifying reassignments, subject to availability.

🧠

Employee Assistance Program

The FIB's Employee Assistance Program (EAP) provides free, confidential counseling services for all staff dealing with stress, anxiety, relationship issues, or the psychological aftermath of witnessing things that cannot be described in this document. Up to 8 sessions per year are covered at no cost. All EAP sessions are confidential and will not be shared with supervisors. Sessions may be reviewed for counterintelligence purposes by a separate office that is not HR.

Confidentiality is maintained within the limits of the law and internal security protocol SA-FIB-7712.
🏆

Awards & Recognition

The FIB recognizes exceptional performance through a robust awards program including the Director's Award for Excellence, the San Andreas Medal of Valor, and the rarely-awarded Classified Commendation — which is real, cannot be displayed publicly, and cannot be listed on a resume, but looks very impressive in a sealed envelope that you are not allowed to open. Length-of-service pins are awarded at 5, 10, 20, and 30 years. The 30-year pin comes with a handshake from the Deputy Director and the sincere hope that you have not kept a journal.

⭐

Plausible Deniability

Perhaps the Bureau's most valued benefit. FIB employment provides institutional cover for a broad range of activities that, in a civilian context, would raise significant legal and ethical questions. Agents operating within the scope of authorized operations enjoy robust legal representation, indemnification from personal liability, and a chain of command willing to describe your actions to the press as "consistent with Bureau policy" for as long as is operationally convenient. Coverage applies to authorized operations only. Unauthorized operations are your problem.

The Bureau reserves the right to redefine what constitutes an "authorized operation" retroactively.
